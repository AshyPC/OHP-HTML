Hope in Christ means grieving well

Loss is part of life in a broken world.
Grief is normal… A lament gives words to what is in the heart.

1. Grief increases our longing & motivates us to action (vv.17-21).

We are not to be shoddy in our grieving.
As believers, we need learn to grieve well, so grief does not turn inward in a destructive way, but is turned outward in dependence on Lord & action. Let our grief increase our longing for the glory God has promised, & motivate us to work for it.

2. Grief is gracious – it thinks the best (vv.22-23)

Grief is gracious because it is the flip side of love – i.e. it arises from the loss of what is loved.
Failure to be gracious (delighting in the misfortune of others) arises from pride – we think we are more deserving. Such thinking shows we have not understood the gospel. The gospel leaves no room to delight in the misfortune of others.

3. Grief reveals what is in our hearts, because we grieve most over what we love most (vv.24-27)

David grieved the defeat and the death of Saul, but his greatest grief was for the loss of Jonathan.
Their relationship was about covenant devotion that had its source in the Lord and rested in Him.
Matthew Henry: “The more we love, the more we grieve.”
What grieves you? Our grief reveals what we love most.

Faith under our grieving.

1 Thessalonians 4:13: Brothers and sisters, we do not want you to be uninformed about those who sleep in death, so that you do not grieve like the rest of mankind, who have no hope.
Paul Tripp warns : “…suffering has a way of entering our door, and when it does, we’d better have handholds to reach for that keep us from being swept away by the power, discouragement, and helplessness of it all.”
We cope because we hold on to Jesus and His promises and the hope that comes from an horizon that stretches into eternity.
Isaiah 53:4: Jesus “…has borne our griefs & carried our sorrows…”
He redeems our sorrow and suffering and builds glory from it!
Jesus “bore our griefs and carried our sorrows” on the cross, and so, like David,
  • our grieving can increase our longing and move us to action;
  • in our grieving we can be gracious; and
  • in our grieving, may we testify to the love of that which is most lovely.

PP 2: Behold The Lamb

1. Behold the Lamb who bears our sins away, slain for us and we remember the promise made that all who come in faith find forgiveness at the cross.
So we share in this bread of life, and we drink of His sacrifice as a sign of our bonds of peace around the table of the King.
2. The body of our Saviour Jesus Christ, torn for you eat and remember: The wounds that heal, the death that brings us life paid the price to make us one.
So we share in this bread of life, and we drink of His sacrifice as a sign of our bonds of love around the table of the King.
3. The blood that cleanses every stain of sin, shed for you drink and remember He drained death's cup that all may enter in to receive the life of God.
So we share in this bread of life, and we drink of His sacrifice as a sign of our bonds of grace around the table of the King.
4. And so with thankfulness and faith we rise to respond and to remember our call to follow in the steps of Christ as His body here on earth.
As we share in His suffering, we proclaim Christ will come again! And we'll join in the feast of heaven around the table of the King. © Keith & Kristyn Getty & Stuart Townend, CCLI 242927
