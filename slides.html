While We Wait

The concept of waiting is almost extinct.

1. The Delay of His Return

It is a long delay as it is still ongoing.
We feel frustrated because it seems like God is not answering our prayers.

How long will the return of Jesus

be delayed?

We cannot be sure because Jesus is also not sure. Jesus says, "But about that day or hour no one knows, not even the angels in heaven, nor the Son, but only the Father." (24:36)

Before Jesus comes, everything will

be normal.

We will be just living our everyday lives: going to school, university, and work. We will be having our routines. People will going to the gym. People will be getting married.

Judgement.

"Two men will be in the field; one will be taken and the other left. Two women will be grinding with a hand mill; one will be taken and other left." (24:40-41)
We are not to assume that baptism, leadership status, title, and the quantity of service to the church automatically means being adopted into God’s family.

2. Keep Watch

"Keep watch, because you do not know on what day your Lord will come." (24:42)

a. Anticipate Jesus’ Return

The day of the Lord’s return is an exciting time!
"So you also must be ready, because the Son of Man will come at an hour when you do not expect him." (24:44)

b. Being Prepared

To be prepared means trusting Jesus.
"Heaven and earth will pass away, but my words will never pass away." (24:35)

3. Faithfully Follow

a. Obedience

It will be good for that servant whose master finds him doing so when he returns. (24:36)
Our faith and Jesus calling is not about being legalistic. Rather he is asking for a response, a transformation of the heart.
The argument now would not just be "My master is staying away a long time," anymore, but rather people are now saying, "My Master does not exist."
"No one can serve two masters. Either you will hate the one and love the other, or you will be devoted to the one and despise the other. You cannot serve both God and money." (6:24)

b. Good Stewards

"The man’s eyes are sparkling. He is bubbling over with enthusiasm, is thoroughly thrilled, and as it were, invites his master to start counting." -William Hendriksen-
"Master, I knew that you are a hard man, harvesting where you have not sown and gathering where you have not scattered seed. So I was afraid and went out and hid your gold in the ground. See, here is what belongs to you." (25:24-25)
The way the last servant viewed his master is the way the world views Jesus.
Do you acknowledge that everything that we have is owned by God? Do you acknowledge everything is graciously given our Father?
