From the shifting shadows of the earth we will lift our eyes to Him, where steady arms of mercy reach to gather children in.
Rejoice, Rejoice! Let every tongue rejoice! One heart, one voice; O Church of Christ, rejoice!

Rejoice, Rejoice! Let every tongue rejoice! One heart, one voice; O Church of Christ, rejoice!

God's gracious king

The qualities of God’s King are also the qualities of the Kingdom, and therefore the way of life of all who belong to it.

1. God’s King does not grasp at power (vv.1-3)

David was content to reign over one tribe in Judah for 7 ½ years.
Jesus, “being in very nature God, did not consider equality with God something to be grasped…” (Phil 2:6). He was willing to walk the way of the cross to the throne.
What about us? Are we dissatisfied but content in the present because we trust God for a certain future.

2. God’s King extends grace to his enemies (vv.4-7)

David was determined to show the people of Jabesh Gilead exceptional loving-kindness, because that is what they showed to Saul as the Lord’s anointed.
Jesus shows us exceptional loving-kindness. “…God demonstrates his own love for us in this: While we were still sinners, Christ died for us.” (Rom 5:8)
Jesus says to us: “I tell you: Love your enemies and pray for those who persecute you…” (Matt 5:44)

3. God’s King will draw opposition from rival kingdoms (vv.8-11)

“Meanwhile, Abner son of Ner, the commander of Saul’s army, had taken Ish-Bosheth son of Saul … He him king over Gilead, Ashuri and Jezreel, and also over Ephraim, Benjamin and all Israel.” (2 Sam 2:8–9)
Jesus “came to that which was his own, but his own did not receive him.” (John 1:11)
“In this world you will have trouble. But take heart! I have overcome the world.” (John 16:33)

Rej 246: Crown Him With Many Crowns

1. Crown Him with many crowns, the Lamb upon His throne, while heaven's eternal anthem drowns all music but its own! Awake, my soul, and sing of Him who died to be your Saviour and your matchless King through all eternity.
2. Crown Him the Lord of life, triumphant from the grave, who rose victorious from the strife for those He came to save: His glories now we sing who died and reigns on high; who died eternal life to bring and lives that death may die.
3. Crown Him the Lord of love, who shows His hands and side - those wounds yet visible above in beauty glorified. No angel in the sky can fully bear that sight, but downward bends his burning eye at mysteries so bright.
4. Crown Him the Lord of peace - His Kingdom is at hand; from pole to pole let warfare cease and Christ rule every land! A city stands on high, His glory it displays, and there the nations 'Holy' cry in joyful hymns of praise.
