God at the centre

The ark…
  1. was a visual reminder of God’s presence with them
  2. represented the covenant between God and Israel
  3. was at the heart of worship and spoke of God’s mercy and His laws

1. How God comes

If Israel is to be the Lord’s people, the ark (representing God’s presence) must be at the centre of their existence.
2 Samuel 6:1–2 David again brought together out of Israel chosen men, thirty thousand in all. He and all his men set out from Baalah of Judah to bring up from there the ark of God, which is called by the Name, the name of the Lord Almighty, who is enthroned between the cherubim that are on the ark.

a) God is not safe

2 Samuel 6:7 The Lord’s anger burned against Uzzah because of his irreverent act; therefore God struck him down and he died there beside the ark of God.
2 Samuel 6:8 Then David was angry because the Lord’s wrath had broken out against Uzzah, and to this day that place is called Perez Uzzah.

b) God is holy

The problem is they decided to carry the ark around in the same way the Philistines had, ignoring God’s instructions.
Numbers 4:15 “After Aaron and his sons have finished covering the holy furnishings and all the holy articles, and when the camp is ready to move, the Kohathites are to come to do the carrying. But they must not touch the holy things or they will die.”
2 Samuel 6:9 David was afraid of the Lord that day and said, “How can the ark of the Lord ever come to me?”
Perhaps, like David, we want God at the centre of our lives, but we try to do it on our terms. Our hearts may twist even the things of God to make them about us. God is not safe, but He is holy, and He is good.

c) God’s presence is blessing

2 Samuel 6:11 The ark of the Lord remained in the house of Obed-Edom the Gittite for three months, and the Lord blessed him and his entire household.
God does not confront David’s attitude, but in love and patience he woos him back to Himself.
In the cross of Jesus, we see the extraordinary lengths to which God will go.

2. Responding to God’s presence

a) with humility and rejoicing David makes sacrifices – we can only draw near to God through a sacrifice for our sin. David humbles himself, laying aside his royal robes. David worships with unrestrained joy!
b) with dignity and misery Michal stands in contrast. She despised David. For her, the kingship is about dignity and demanding respect. But standing on our own dignity will always block the way to God.
Chapter 6 is about having the right foundation It is about God’s presence at the centre of everything It is about God’s grace that makes it possible, even when our own hearts would give up And it is about our absolute JOY in worship when He is at the centre
