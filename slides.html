Responding to the Kingdom

1. Abner – opposing the kingdom.

Abner thought the best future was in a rival kingdom.   A kingdom tied to the family of Saul.   A kingdom where he could exert his influence.
Abner opposed the Lord’s ‘messiah’. We do too when:
  • we think the Kingdom can be ours by our own efforts;
  • we know what Jesus says but fail to live it out;
  • fail to submit our lives and desires to Jesus’ sovereign rule.

2. Abner – seeking advantage from the kingdom.

2 Samuel 3:9-10 “May God deal with Abner, be it ever so severely, if I do not do for David what the Lord promised him on oath and transfer the kingdom from the house of Saul and establish David’s throne over Israel and Judah from Dan to Beersheba.”
2 Samuel 3:12 Then Abner sent messengers on his behalf to say to David, “Whose land is it? Make an agreement with me, and I will help you bring all Israel over to you.”
Like Abner, do we sometimes seek the Kingdom for our own advantage? Are there times when we turn to Jesus only because of what we want from Him? How easy it is to shift the ‘balance’ in our understanding of the gospel to make it ‘about us.’

3. Joab – subverting the kingdom.

Joab is also acting for his own glory.
The murder of Abner is partly about revenge, but probably also about removing a rival for fear of losing his position as commander (see also 2 Samuel 20).
To what extent are our motives pure as we serve our King?
It is easy for pride to rise in our hearts, to desire power or the praise of others, and make serving Jesus about US.
John Woodhouse commented: “Some sought to hasten it. Some sought to defeat it. Some sought to turn the kingdom to their own advantage. But none of them was good enough, wise enough, or powerful enough to accomplish what they intended.” The Kingdom may only be attained at the invitation of the King and in the grace that He gives.
The Kingdom can never be twisted to our own advantage; in fact:
  • it is our desire for own advantage that we surrender to enter it;
  • it is desire for own glory that we swap to bask in Jesus’s glory;
  • it is building our own kingdom that we give up to seek His Kingdom;
  • it is things that corrode, decay, waste away & disappoint, that we swap for eternal weight of glory & life with the King Himself.
